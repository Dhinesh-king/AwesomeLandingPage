Programmer's Day 2021

Do you know the importance of the number 256? Not everyone understands that it’s the building block of so much in our everyday lives. The number 256 is the number of distinct values that can be represented with a byte, a value well-known to programmers. Not only is it a special number, but this year it’s a special day - the 256th day of this year kicks off a week you won’t want to miss.

From September 13 – 17, Cognizant will host its annual Programmers’ Week. The week long event is designed specifically for people like YOU - global developers and software engineers who can share and benefit from invaluable professional development, training, and new technology insights and ideas.

KeyNote Speaker:

The keynote speakers for this year’s Programmers’ Week come from a variety of backgrounds and will speak about topics from women in engineering, the future of software and machine learning. Some are names you may recognize, others are bringing their expertise to us from places like Aston Martin and NASA.

  1. Brian Humphries, CEO of Cognizant: Introducing Game of Pods
  2. Otmar Szafnauer, CEO and Team Principal of Aston Martin Cognizant Formula One Team: A Personal Journey of Innovation, Technology and Racing
  3. Nagin Cox, Spacecraft Operations Engineer: Go Beyond

You can also look forward to several panels including Women in Engineering and The Future of Software. In addition to the keynotes, Programmers’ Week will include five days of Tech Talks. Each day has a different theme, so on one day you can deepen your knowledge and the next you can learn something new.

What To expect?:

The theme for this year’s Programmers’ Week is GO BEYOND. It’s meant to inspire global technologists like you to be bolder, more creative and to ‘go beyond’ your current thinking and skill sets. Not only will the event showcase how our own talent communities go beyond to set new standards for excellence, but it will also show how inclusivity and environmental efforts in 2022 will help us all go beyond.

For the first time ever, Programmers’ Week will include Product Day, a day dedicated to learning about all things product-related, including demos, discussions on new product technologies and approaches and successful implementations.
